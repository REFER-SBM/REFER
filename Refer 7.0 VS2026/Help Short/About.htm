1.2 About

REFER is a Sketch-Based Modeling application under continuos development by the research group REGEO
Current version is REFER 7.0

The version history is as follows:

REFER 1.0 was developed as part of the following capstone project:

J.V. Andreu (2000). Desarrollo, implementación y prueba de un algoritmo de reconstrucción de objetos a partir de una representación axonométrica, utilizando técnicas de optimización. Facultad de Informática. Univ. Politecnica de Valencia

The software was a C implementation, in a Windows environment, of the optimization approach for line-drawing reconstruction proposed by Lipson and Shpitalni in 1996.
doi: 10.1145/1281500.1281556.

Major improvements:
1) Open code.
2) Interactive interface.
3) Expandable database.

REFER 2.0 was a direct result of the following PhD Dissertation:

J. Conesa (2001). Reconstrucción Geométrica de sólidos utilizando técnicas de optimización Tesis doctoral. Universidad Politecnica de Cartagena http://hdl.handle.net/10317/479. DOI: 10.31428/10317/479.

Major improvements:
1) Upgraded detection of regularities.
2) Implementation and tuning of two variants of Simulated Annealing algorithms. (Conesa J., Contero M. y Company P. Comportamiento de los algoritmos de optimización en la reconstrucción geométrica de sólidos. Anales de Ingeniería Mecánica, Año 13, vol. 1, 2000, pp. 425-430).
3) Implementation of five direct inflation approaches: i) axonometric, ii) levels, iii) cavalier, iv) Marill's MSDA and v) random. (Conesa J., Company P. and Gomis J.M. Initial Modeling Strategies for Geometrical Reconstruction Optimization-Based Approaches. Proc. 11th ADM Int. Conf. on Design Tools and Methods in Industrial Engineering. Volume A, 1999. ISBN 88-900081-9-9, pp. 161-171).

REFER 3.0 was released as a result of the following PhD Dissertation:

A. Piquer (2003) Percepción artificial de dibujo lineales. Tesis Doctoral. Universitat Jaume I.
http://hdl.handle.net/10803/10554. ISBN 8468870617.

Major improvements:
1) Improvements in face detection (new approach derived from Courter and Brewer's).
2) Bilateral symmetry detection in 2D line-drawings. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-25977-0_13.
3) 2D offline refinement through optimization.

REFER 4.0 was an unpublished update developed throughout 2004

While searching for a smart sketch system (https://doi.org/10.1007/3-540-37620-8_6), F. Naya added the sketching capability through the CALI Library
(MJ Fonseca et al. Cali: An online scribble recognizer for calligraphic interfaces. 2002 AAAI Spring Symposium)
Major improvements in versions 3 and 4 were published in:
Company P., Contero M., Conesa J. and Piquer A. (2004) An optimisation-based reconstruction engine for 3D modelling by sketching. Computers & Graphics 28(6), 955-979. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cag.2004.08.007.


REFER 5 was a major update, which started in 2009

5.1 P. Company (2009). Update to C++ and MFC, plus new database
5.2 P. Company (2010). Code structured by classes and new execution flow
5.3 P.A.C. Varley and P. Company (2010). A new algorithm for finding faces in wireframes. (http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.cad.2009.11.008)
5.4 P. Company (2011). Adaptation of Corner detection approach by Xiong and LaViola (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cag.2010.06.008)
5.5 P. Company and P.A.C. Varley (2011). Features detection (https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-13544-6_14).
5.6 P. Company and P.A.C. Varley (2012). Improvements in detection of cues.
5.7 P. Company, R. Plumed and P.A.C. Varley (2013). Sketch editor and vectorization module.
5.8 R. Plumed, P. Company and P.A.C. Varley (2013). Improvements in features detection.

REFER 6 was a result of the following PhD Dissertation:

R. Plumed (2014) Sobre la percepción de indicios en bocetos de ingeniería y su aplicación al modelado basado en bocetos. Tesis Doctoral. Universitat Jaume I. http://hdl.handle.net/10803/286032. ISBN 978-84-606-6619-6.

Major improvements:
6.1 New vectorization code (https://doi.org/10.1007/s00371-015-1099-6).
6.2 Detection of vanishing points and grouping of parallel edges (https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-662-44854-0_7).
6.3 Improvements on perceptually-based detection of features (https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-33191-6_55, https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-31745-3_5).
6.4 Improvements on mirror symmetry detection in 2D line-drawings (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cag.2016.05.013).
6.5 New method to obtain a 3D CSG models from a 2D engineering wireframe sketch which depicts a polyhedral shape (http://dx.doi.org/10.2312/egsh.20141008).
Major improvements in version 6.5 were published in:

Plumed R, Company P, Varley P, Martin R. (2014) CSG feature trees from engineering sketches of polyhedral shapes. In: Eurographics 2014, short papers. 2014, p. 33–6. (URI: http://hdl.handle.net/10234/149685. DOI: 10.2312/egsh.20141008).

Plumed R., Varley P.A.C., Company P. and Martin R.R.(2022) Extracting datums to reconstruct CSG Models from 2D engineering sketches of polyhedral shapes. Computers & Graphics 102, 349-359 (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cag.2021.10.013).


REFER 7 included a major update to the help file, providing a clearer explanation of the application's new approach, which places greater emphasis on the reconstruction of procedural models.

Major improvements in version 7.0 were published in:

Camba J.D., Company P. and Naya F. (2022) Sketch-Based Modeling in Mechanical Engineering Design: Current Status and Opportunities. Computer-Aided Design. Volume 150, September 2022, 103283. (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cad.2022.103283).
