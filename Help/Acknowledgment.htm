Acknowledgment

This book compiles over two decades of the authors' knowledge and experience in Sketch-Based Modeling (SBM) for engineering design. Rather than providing a theoretical description of the problem, it focuses on demonstrating different methods and algorithms through interactive implementations. This approach allows readers and users to experiment firsthand, making the learning process more practical and engaging.

The book is closely connected to REFER, a software application developed over the years to experiment with and test various SBM methods and approaches. To make both the book and REFER more useful to readers, the authors have gathered, revised, and updated the source code. Additionally, the code has been integrated with the "help" manual to enhance usability and understanding.

This process demanded countless hours devoted to identifying and fixing the many bugs that inevitably accumulate over time in an academic software application. Despite our best efforts, some issues likely remain. Nevertheless, we decided to release the current version, confident that—errors aside—it will still be valuable to readers interested in the evolution and current state of sketch-based modeling in engineering design.

The authors have made every effort in the preparation of this book to ensure the accuracy of the information. However, the information contained in this book is sold without warranty, either exppressed or implied. Any projections concerning the future of SBM that are interwoven throughout the explanations of the functioning of the different methods are purely the authos' opinions.

Naturally, this work builds upon a foundation of prior collective efforts. The chronological sequence of contributions to earlier versions of the source code is outlined in the "About" section, along with citations of relevant publications that emerged from these collaborative efforts over time. The authors would like to highlight and acknowledge the important contributions of Juan Vicente Andreu, Julian Conesa, Ana Piquer, Ferran Naya, Peter Varley, and Raquel Plumed, as well as the fruitful collaborations with Manuel Contero, to the different versions of the software which have enriched REFER's ever-evolving code. It has been an honor and a pleasure to collaborate with all of them. Without their respective contributions, REFER could not have reached this seventh version, which is now available to readers as part of this handbook.
